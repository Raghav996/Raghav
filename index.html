portfolio

A portfolio is a curated collection of an individual’s work, achievements, and experiences. It can serve various purposes across different fields, from showcasing a professional’s skills to documenting an academic journey. Portfolios can be physical or digital, and they are increasingly used in educational and professional settings to demonstrate capability and growth. Here’s an overview of what portfolios are and the advantages they offer.

What is a Portfolio?


Definition: A portfolio is a comprehensive compilation of documents, samples, and artifacts that reflect an individual’s skills, accomplishments, and learning journey. It often includes work samples, project reports, case studies, certificates, and reflections on personal and professional development.

Types of Portfolios:


Professional Portfolio:
Showcases a person’s work achievements, skills, and experiences to potential employers or clients. It may include resumes, cover letters, work samples, and references.

Academic Portfolio:
Used by students to document their learning progress, achievements, and reflections. It often includes assignments, projects, grades, and self-assessments.

Creative Portfolio:
Common in fields like art, design, and writing, this type of portfolio highlights creative work such as artwork, design projects, and writing samples.
